Logistics AI

Most Logistics-AI is solving yesterday's problems with yesterday's data. Train a model. Deploy. Pray nothing changes. Something always changes.

A single Ant is an idiot. A colony is a genius. No CEO central command. No retraining. Just thousands of individuals following simple rules, yet together they build bridges, find shortest paths, and adapt when you destroy their world. Ants figured out Distributed Intelligence 100 million years ago.

That's not biology. That's Physics written in Chemistry. Pheromones diffusing like heat. Positive feedback loops amplifying what works. Self-organization that would make any AI Engineer struggle!

I stripped it down to First-Principles. Built a simulation where every ant follows three rules: Explore until you find something, Mark the path on your way back, Follow the strongest trails. Just 23 Variables.

That's it.

Watch the video simulation above, which I built with Python (NumPy & PyGame). Blue scouts explore. Orange carriers reinforce. A perfect supply chain emerges from pure chaos. Drop an obstacle? They reroute in seconds. Move the food? They find it again.

Now map that to your world. The pheromones are your data streams. The scouts are your drones. The workers are your trucks. The obstacles are whatever chaos the world throws at you.

Commercial delivery. Oil and gas logistics. Forces' supply chains. Same engine. Different context.

The tools will change. The physics won't. I build on what works.

The ants showed us the blueprint. It's time we used it.
